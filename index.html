Nirjhar Das

I am an incoming MTech (Research) student at the Dept. of CSA, IISc Bangalore under the guidance of Prof Siddharth Barman. For the next two years, I hope to explore new areas in Learning Theory, Game Theory and Computational Social Choice and work on interesting problems at the intersection of these areas. I am excited about this new journey.

I graduated with a B.Tech in Electrical Engineering from IIT Delhi. I have completed my thesis under Prof. Arpan Chattopadhyay in areas of Reinforcement Learning and Multi-Armed Bandits which was selected for the Best BTech Project Award. Alongside my major, I have also completed a Specialization in Cognitive and Intelligent Systems (AI Focus Area) in the Dept. of EE, IIT Delhi.

From August 2023 to June 2024, I worked as a Research Intern at Microsoft Research India under Gaurav Sinha. At MSRI, I worked on problems related to Multi-Armed Bandits in Large Action Spaces and under various constraints.

My research interest spans theoretical and applied areas in Decision-Making Under Uncertainty and High Dimensional Probability. Recently, I have also started exploring Causal Inference. I am also interested in other areas involving probability, algorithms and optimization. My broader research motivation is to understand the interaction of several agents and study emergent intelligent behaviours from simpler systems. Using these insights, I want to design agents that can learn in ways similar to humans, that is, agents that rely on abstraction and are data-efficient.

I also find it interesting to frame challenges related to large-scale systems like LLMs and Robotics in a succinct theoretical problem and propose principled but practical algorithms with theoretical guarantees.
