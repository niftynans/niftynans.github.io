Nirjhar Das

I am a fresh graduate with a B.Tech in Electrical Engineering from IIT Delhi. I have completed my thesis under Prof. Arpan Chattopadhyay in areas of Reinforcement Learning and Multi-Armed Bandits which was selected for the Best BTech Project Award. Alongside my major, I have also completed a Specialization in Cognitive and Intelligent Systems (AI Focus Area) in the Dept. of EE, IIT Delhi. Currently, I am working as a Research Intern at Microsoft Research India under Gaurav Sinha. At MSRI, I am working in Reinforcement Learning in Large Action Spaces.

Before joining MSRI, I spent 3 wonderful months as a Research Assistant in the Approximation Algorithms Lab led by Prof Siddharth Barman at the Dept. of CSA, IISc Bangalore, where I worked in Participatory Budgeting with Bandit Feedback.

My research interest spans theoretical and applied areas in Decision-Making Under Uncertainty and High Dimensional Probability. I am also interested in other areas involving probability, algorithms and optimization. My broader research motivation is to understand the interaction of several agents and study emergent intelligent behaviours from simpler systems. Using these insights, I want to design agents that can learn in ways similar to humans, that is, agents that rely on abstraction and are data-efficient.

I am also working with Souradip Chakroborty from University of Maryland in the area of Reinforcement Learning with Human Feedback. Before that, I have also worked with Prof. Rahul Garg, CSE, IIT Delhi in the area of computer vision.
